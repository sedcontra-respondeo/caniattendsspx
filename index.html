    • Objection 3. The Vatican’s Grant of Faculties for Confessions and Marriages Proves That the Society Is Not Suspended.
    • Objection 4. The SSPX Was Not Validly Suppressed.
    • On the contrary, (A Papally Imposed Suspension a divinis , and an Automatic Suspension Under Canon 1388).
    • Reply to Objection 1. Archbishop Lefebvre Knowingly Defied a Suspension That He Publicly Admitted, and Thus Priests Ordained Afterwards Cannot Argue Ignorance.
    • Reply to Objection 2. The SSPX Cannot Avail Itself of the Necessity Defense if Other Means Were Available.
    • Reply to Objection 3. The Faculties Only Benefit the Faithful; They Do Not Imply Canonical Regularity.
    • Reply to Objection 4. The 1975 Suppression Was Never Overturned — and Lefebvre's Own Recourse to the Apostolic Signatura Failed.
  • Whether the Episcopal Consecrations Performed by Archbishop Lefebvre on June 30, 1988 Constituted a Schismatic Act.
    • Objection 1. No Schismatic Intent.
    • Objection 2. Justified by State of Necessity.
    • Objection 3. The Protocol of Accord Demonstrates the Holy See in Principle Accepted the Consecration of a Bishop.
    • Objection 4. The Canons on Episcopal Consecration Are Merely Disciplinary, Not of Divine or Dogmatic Law.
    • Objection 5. The 1988 Consecrations Do Not Meet the Definition of Schism.
    • Objection 6. Canon Law Treats Illicit Consecration and Schism as Distinct Offenses.
    • Objection 7. Consecration Confers Office, Not Jurisdiction.
    • On the contrary, (Declared Schism in Ecclesia Dei Adflicta and in the Decree of Excommunication).
    • Reply to Objection 1. Intent Does Not Change the Object of the Act.
    • Reply to Objection 2. Epikeia Fails When the Legislator Is Present and Refused.
    • Reply to Objection 3. The Protocol Died When Lefebvre Repudiated It.
    • Reply to Objection 4. Episcopal Consecration Is Divine Law, Not Positive Law.
    • Reply to Objection 5. The Act Itself Proves the Schismatic Intent.
    • Reply to Objection 6. Independent Authority, Not Jurisdiction, Is the Schismatic Core.
    • Reply to Objection 7. Hierarchical Communion Is Constitutive, Not a Formality.
  • Whether the Episcopal Consecrations Performed by the Society of Saint Pius X on July 1, 2026 Likewise Constituted a Schismatic Act.
    • Objection 1. Institutional Extinction Creates Genuine Necessity.
    • Objection 2. Openness to Dialogue Shows No Refusal of Submission.
    • Objection 3. Thirty-Eight Years of Unresolved Theological Dispute.
    • Objection 4. Rome's Double Standard in China and Ukraine.
    • Objection 5. The Crisis in the Church Is Worse Than in 1988.

    • Reply to Objection 1. Necessity Fails When the Offered Means Was Refused.
    • Reply to Objection 2. Proceeding Anyway Is Not Offset by Talk of Dialogue.
    • Reply to Objection 3. Ongoing Dispute Does Not Make the Charge False.
    • Reply to Objection 4. Inconsistent Enforcement Does Not Void the Canon.
    • Reply to Objection 5. Necessity Admits No Degrees; the Necessity Has Lessened Since 1988, Not Grown; and the Society Cannot Be Judge of Its Own Necessity.
  • Whether the latae sententiae Excommunications Declared by the Dicastery for the Doctrine of the Faith on 2 July 2026 Were Validly Incurred and Canonically Effective.
    • Objection 1. Illicit Episcopal Consecration Is an Offense Against Sacraments, Not Schism.
    • Objection 2. The Excommunications Are Invalid Because Grave Necessity Reduces or Excuses From the Penalties of Law.
    • Objection 3. The Decree Exceeds the Canons Invoked.
    • Objection 4. The SSPX Does Not Have a Schismatic Intention.
    • Objection 5. The July 11, 2026 Recourse Suspends Any Canonical Penalties.
    • On the contrary, (The 1996 PCILT Note, Readopted in 2026, Has Already Judged the Necessity, Validity, and Mitigating-Circumstance Questions).
    • Reply to Objection 1. The Location of the Canons for Consecration of a Bishop and Schism Does Not Alone Determine the Nature of the Crime.
    • Reply to Objection 2. Canons 1323–1324 Do Not Excuse an Act Against a Personal Papal Prohibition.
    • Reply to Objection 3. A Declaratory Decree Needs No Individualized Process — the Law Is Clear, Not Doubtful.
    • Reply to Objection 4. Pertinacity Is Established Objectively, Not by Professed Good Will.
    • Reply to Objection 5. Suspending the Decree's Declaration Does Not Undo the ipso facto Penalty — and the Recourse Itself Concedes the Forum.
  • Whether the Faithful Commit Sin by Receiving Holy Communion from the SSPX.
    • Objection 1. Because a Priest Says Mass in the Name of the Church, Faithful Do Not Sin by Receiving From a Sinful Priest.
    • Objection 2. Hawaii Five and Msgr. Perl Decisions Say This Is Permitted.
    • Objection 3. Due to Necessity, Supplied Jurisdiction, and/or epikeia Attendance Is Licit.
    • Objection 4. Pope Francis’ Faculties for Confessions and Marriage Prove Church Tolerates SSPX Sacraments.
    • Objection 5. If the Church Permits Reception from Eastern Orthodox, She A Fortiori Permits Sacraments From the SSPX.
    • Objection 6. Canon 1335 Lifts Censures When Faithful Legitimately Request the Sacraments.

    • Reply to Objection 2. The Hawaii Five Ruling, Perl Letter, and PCILT Address Schism and Not the Thomistic Principle of Communication in the Sin of the Non-Tolerated Minister.
    • Reply to Objection 3. Probable Opinion Does Not Work When the Competent Authority Rules Otherwise.

    • Reply to Objection 5. Canon 844 Does Not Apply to the SSPX Situation.
    • Reply to Objection 6. Canon 1335 §2 Answers Hidden Censures — the SSPX's Censure Is Public and Declared.
